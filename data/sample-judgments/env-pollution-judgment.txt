JUDGMENT

DATE: 15th January 2025

1. This writ petition has been filed by the petitioner, an environmental advocacy group, challenging the inaction of the Karnataka State Pollution Control Board (KSPCB) in addressing severe air and water pollution in the industrial zones of Peenya and Jigani, Bengaluru.

2. The petitioner has raised serious concerns about industrial effluents being discharged into Vrishabhavathi River without adequate treatment, causing significant environmental degradation and health hazards to residents in surrounding areas.

FINDINGS:

3. After careful consideration of the evidence presented, including the technical reports submitted by environmental experts, this Court finds that the respondents have failed to discharge their statutory duties under the Environment (Protection) Act, 1986 and the Water (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1974.

4. The Court notes with concern that despite repeated complaints since March 2023, the KSPCB has not conducted adequate inspections of the industrial units in question. The latest water quality report dated 10th December 2024 shows alarmingly high levels of heavy metals and chemical oxygen demand (COD) exceeding permissible limits by 400%.

5. It is evident that immediate and stringent action is required to prevent further environmental damage and protect public health.

DIRECTIONS:

6. In light of the above findings, this Court hereby directs as follows:

   a) The Karnataka State Pollution Control Board shall within 30 days from the date of this judgment, conduct a comprehensive inspection of all industrial units in Peenya and Jigani industrial areas and submit a detailed report to this Court.

   b) The State of Karnataka shall establish a Special Task Force comprising officials from KSPCB, Karnataka State Industrial Development Corporation (KSIDC), and environmental experts within 15 days to monitor and enforce compliance with environmental norms.

   c) All industrial units found to be violating pollution norms must install or upgrade their effluent treatment plants to meet the prescribed standards within 90 days. The KSPCB shall issue show cause notices to defaulting units within 7 days of inspection.

   d) The respondent board is directed to implement a real-time water quality monitoring system at five critical points along the Vrishabhavathi River within 120 days. The data from these monitoring stations shall be made publicly accessible through a web portal.

7. The KSPCB must file a compliance report before this Court every 60 days until full compliance is achieved, detailing the actions taken and results obtained. The first such report shall be filed by 15th March 2025.

8. The State Government shall allocate adequate funds, not less than Rs. 50 crores, for the immediate commencement of river rejuvenation work, including removal of accumulated silt and industrial waste. A detailed action plan and timeline for this work shall be submitted to the Court within 45 days.

9. Any industrial unit that continues to discharge untreated effluents after the expiry of the 90-day period shall face immediate closure and prosecution under the relevant environmental laws. The KSPCB is empowered to seal such units without further reference to this Court.

10. The Court appoints an expert committee consisting of Dr. Ramesh Kumar (Environmental Scientist), Prof. Anjali Sharma (Water Resources Management), and Shri Prakash Rao (retd. Chief Engineer, KSPCB) to oversee the implementation of these directions and submit quarterly progress reports.

11. The registry shall forward a copy of this judgment to the Chief Secretary, Government of Karnataka, and the Chairman, Karnataka State Pollution Control Board, for immediate compliance.

12. This petition is disposed of with the above directions. All parties shall bear their own costs.

13. The matter shall be listed for review on 1st May 2025 to assess the progress of compliance.
