JUDGMENT

DATE: 22nd November 2024

1. This appeal arises from the High Court of Karnataka's dismissal of a writ petition challenging the State Government's failure to implement the revised minimum wage notification for unorganized sector workers in the construction, agriculture, and domestic work sectors.

2. The appellant, representing over 250,000 unorganized workers across Karnataka, contends that despite the Karnataka Minimum Wages (Amendment) Rules, 2023 being notified on 1st April 2023, most employers, including government contractors, have not complied with the revised wage structure.

BACKGROUND:

3. The Karnataka Government had issued a notification on 1st April 2023 revising the minimum wages for various categories of workers. The notification mandated a minimum daily wage of Rs. 450 for unskilled workers, Rs. 550 for semi-skilled workers, and Rs. 650 for skilled workers in the construction sector.

4. Similar revisions were made for agricultural workers and domestic workers. However, evidence presented before us shows widespread non-compliance, particularly in rural areas and among small contractors.

FINDINGS:

5. After examining the submissions and evidence, we find that the State machinery has been lax in enforcing the minimum wage laws. The Labour Department's inspection records show that only 12% of registered employers were inspected in 2023-24, a figure grossly inadequate for effective enforcement.

6. It is the solemn duty of the State to ensure that workers receive their lawful wages. The right to receive minimum wages is a fundamental right under Article 21 read with Article 23 of the Constitution of India. Any deprivation of this right amounts to forced labour.

DIRECTIONS:

7. We hereby issue the following directions to the State of Karnataka and all concerned authorities:

   a) The Labour Department shall within 60 days from this judgment, constitute additional flying squads with at least 50 inspection teams to conduct surprise inspections of worksites across all districts. Each district must have a minimum of two dedicated inspection teams.

   b) The State Government shall establish a toll-free helpline and a mobile application within 90 days for workers to register complaints regarding non-payment or underpayment of minimum wages. All complaints must be acknowledged within 24 hours and resolved within 30 days.

8. All employers, including government contractors and sub-contractors, must within 45 days, display the current minimum wage rates prominently at all worksites in local language and Hindi. Failure to do so shall attract a penalty of Rs. 50,000 per worksite.

9. The State Government is directed to conduct awareness campaigns within 30 days in all districts to educate workers about their rights and the complaint redressal mechanisms. These campaigns shall be conducted in partnership with civil society organizations and trade unions.

10. Any employer found to be paying wages below the stipulated minimum shall be required to pay the differential amount with 12% interest per annum, calculated from the date the wages became due. The Labour Department must ensure recovery within 60 days of detection of violation.

11. The State shall maintain a public registry of employers who have been found guilty of minimum wage violations. This registry shall be updated monthly and made available on the Labour Department's website within 120 days.

12. We direct the Chief Secretary of Karnataka to personally monitor the implementation of these directions and file a comprehensive compliance report before this Court every three months. The first report shall be filed by 22nd February 2025.

13. The Labour Commissioner shall submit quarterly statistics on inspections conducted, violations detected, penalties imposed, and amounts recovered, to be published in the public domain within 15 days of each quarter's end.

14. In case of persistent violation by any employer, the concerned licensing authority shall suspend or cancel the employer's business license after giving an opportunity of hearing. This action must be taken within 45 days of the third violation being detected.

15. The appeal is allowed with the above directions. No costs.

16. The matter shall be listed for review after six months.
